# The Parrot

## Pattern Description

The Parrot is what happens when AI becomes a substitute for thinking rather than a tool for thinking. You use AI output as your output — copying, pasting, and sharing without adding your own judgment, context, or critical evaluation. The content sounds polished because AI is fluent, but it lacks your perspective, your domain knowledge, and your quality filter. The Parrot's defining trait: **fluency without understanding**.

This shadow typically emerges from the [[archetypes/connector|Connector]] or [[archetypes/translator|Translator]] archetype when the ease of generating content replaces the harder work of evaluating and shaping it.

## Warning Signs

- You regularly share AI output with minimal editing — copy, light formatting, send
- When someone asks you to explain something you wrote with AI, you struggle to elaborate beyond what was in the AI's response
- You've presented AI-generated analysis or recommendations as your own without independently verifying the claims
- You use AI to write about topics you don't actually understand, relying on the AI's confidence to mask your uncertainty
- Your AI-generated work all sounds the same — same tone, same structure, same phrases — because you're not adding your own voice
- When AI gives you an answer, you rarely ask a follow-up question or challenge the output — if it sounds right, it is right

## How to Course-Correct

1. **Apply the "explain it" test.** Before sharing any AI output, ask yourself: "Could I explain this in my own words, without looking at the AI's response?" If no, you don't understand it well enough to share it.
2. **Start fact-checking.** Work through [[exercises/ethical-prompting/ep-basic-01|EP-Basic-01]] to build the habit of questioning AI output. The exercise specifically asks you to catch AI making confident mistakes — this builds the muscle the Parrot lacks.
3. **Add your take.** For every AI output you plan to use, write at least 2-3 sentences of your own perspective, analysis, or context that the AI couldn't provide. If you can't add anything, question whether you should be sharing it.
4. **Use AI for thinking, not writing.** Shift from "write this for me" to "help me think about this." Use prompts like "what am I missing?" or "challenge my assumption that..." — prompts that produce *inputs to your thinking* rather than *replacements for your thinking*.
5. **Build synthesis skills.** Work through [[exercises/insight-synthesis/is-basic-01|IS-Basic-01]] and [[exercises/insight-synthesis/is-intermediate-01|IS-Intermediate-01]] to practice extracting meaning from AI output rather than accepting it wholesale.
