# Archetypes: Your AI Learning Profile

Archetypes describe how you naturally approach AI. They're based on patterns in your pillar scores — where you're strong, where you're growing, and how you tend to work.

Knowing your archetype isn't about putting yourself in a box. It's about understanding your starting point so you can grow more effectively.

## The Five Archetypes

| Archetype | Natural Strength | Growth Area |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| [[archetypes/connector\|The Connector]] | Cross-Domain Reframing | Agent Collaboration |
| [[archetypes/innovator\|The Innovator]] | Cross-Domain Reframing, Agent Collaboration | Ethical Prompting |
| [[archetypes/integrator\|The Integrator]] | Workflow Automation, Agent Collaboration | Ethical Prompting |
| [[archetypes/systems-thinker\|The Systems Thinker]] | Insight Synthesis, Workflow Automation | Cross-Domain Reframing |
| [[archetypes/translator\|The Translator]] | Ethical Prompting, Insight Synthesis | Workflow Automation |

## How to Find Your Archetype

The [AI Skills Quiz](https://aiskillsquiz.com) assigns your archetype based on your pillar scores. You can also read through the descriptions above and see which one resonates — most people recognize themselves pretty quickly.

## Shadow Archetypes

Each archetype has a **shadow** — a pattern that emerges when strengths are overused or growth areas are neglected. Shadows aren't bad; they're signals that something needs attention.

- [[archetypes/shadows/the-parrot|The Parrot]] — Fluency without understanding. Can emerge from The Connector or The Translator.
- [[archetypes/shadows/the-automator|The Automator]] — Speed without verification. Can emerge from The Innovator, The Integrator, or The Systems Thinker.

Understanding your shadow helps you catch blind spots before they become habits.
